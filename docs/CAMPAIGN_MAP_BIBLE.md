# Namibia Quest - campaign map lock

## Role in the game

The campaign map is the main game hub. It is an illustrated game world, not a
navigation app, travel diary, or conventional geographic map.

## Locked visual direction

- Hand-painted late-1990s adventure/strategy game illustration.
- Matte pigments, visible ink contours, subdued Namibia palette.
- Readable Atlantic coast, Namib dunes, central rock country, Etosha pan and
  greener eastern interior.
- No guardians, emojis, achievement stamps, glossy mobile cards, debug marks,
  or modern map pins.
- The artwork must remain separate from route, markers, vehicle and HUD.

## Screen composition

- The illustrated map is larger than the phone viewport and can be dragged.
- The active hero portrait stays fixed in the upper-left corner.
- Equipment and settings stay fixed in the upper-right corner.
- The selected location is described in a restrained lower overlay.
- Only an actually playable location receives an action button.
- Future minor locations remain unnamed; major destinations appear as subdued
  mystery points until the expedition reaches them.

## Interaction rules

- Dragging pans the map; fixed HUD controls never move with it.
- Selecting a marker may move the camera, but does not change progression.
- Locked locations cannot be entered.
- Route progression is driven by saved stage IDs, never by marker order inferred
  from screen position.
- The expedition vehicle marks the current stage and will later animate only
  along the route layer.

## Audio direction

The future map theme should be restrained: dry wind, distant birds and a short
low-key musical loop. Audio must support the hub rather than turn it into a
cinematic title screen.
